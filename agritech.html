üåø Natural Farming

Zero Budget Natural Farming (ZBNF), or Community Managed Natural Farming (CMNF) as it's known in Andhra Pradesh, is a form of agriculture that avoids all chemical fertilizers and pesticides. It relies on natural inputs that are prepared by the farmer using locally available resources like cow dung, cow urine, jaggery, and pulse flour. It aims to make farming economically viable and climate-resilient.

Key Pillars:

  • Jeevamrutham: A microbial culture made from cow dung, urine, jaggery, and water to improve soil fertility.
  • Beejamrutham: A seed treatment to protect seeds from soil-borne diseases.
  • Acchadana (Mulching): Covering the soil with crop residues to retain moisture and build humus.
  • Waaphasa (Soil Aeration): Maintaining a balance of air and water in the soil.
